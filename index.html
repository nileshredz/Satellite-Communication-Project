Antenna Look Angle

The look angles for the ground station antenna are Azimuth (Az) and Elevation (El) angles. They are required at the antenna so that it points directly at the satellite. Look angles are calculated by considering the elliptical orbit.

These angles change in order to track the satellite. For geostationary orbit, these angles values does not change as the satellites are stationary with respect to earth. Thus large earth stations are used for commercial communications.

Limits of Visibility

The east and west limits of geostationary are visible from any given Earth station. These limits are set by the geographic coordinates of the Earth station and antenna elevation. The lowest elevation is zero (in theory) but in practice, to avoid reception of excess noise from Earth.
